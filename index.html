String Expression Evaluator

By: Pearl Lin

Multi-operator expressions are evaluated by order of PEMDAS. PEMDAS rules are down below. Type in a multi-operator expression and hit evaluate to see your result. The translator takes in your expression as a string, parses it, and solves it automatically!

PEMDAS Rules

1. Parentheses have first precedence


2. Exponents have second precedence


3. Multiplication and division have third precedence, and are evaluated from left to right.


4. Multiplication and division have third precedence, and are evaluated from left to right.


5. Addition and subtraction have last precedence, and are evaluated from left to right.


6. Addition and subtraction have last precedence, and are evaluated from left to right.

Guidelines for Inputting Your Expression

✔ Please use * · x × to denote multiplication. Do not use parentheses () for multiplication


✔ Use either / or ÷ to indicate division


✔ Use the carrot symbol ^ to represent exponentiation.


✔ Use the + symbol for addition and use any of the following symbols: - ˗ − ﹣ － for subtraction & negative numbers


✔ Supported ways of writing parentheses: () [] {}


✔ Remember to pair all the sets of parentheses you write! If the number of opening parentheses and closing parentheses does not equal, an error will show


✔ Only use parentheses to group expressions! If you want to indicate multiplication, make sure to use any of the supported multiplication operators.
Ex. Correct: 2 * (3 + 8) Incorrect: 2(3 + 8)


✔ No variables can be in the expression. This is because the evaulator is programmed to give you an exact numerical result.
